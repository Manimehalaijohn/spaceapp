NASA Missions

SWOT Mission

The Surface Water and Ocean Topography (SWOT) mission is a joint venture between NASA and CNES (the French Space Agency). It aims to provide high-resolution observations of Earth's surface water and its elevation, enabling better understanding and monitoring of the ocean and freshwater bodies such as lakes, rivers, and wetlands.

Interesting fact: SWOT will be the first mission to provide global, high-resolution observations of Earth's surface water, allowing scientists to study how water bodies change over time and function in Earth's water cycle.

To learn more about the SWOT mission, visit the official SWOT mission website.

GRACE Mission

The Gravity Recovery and Climate Experiment (GRACE) mission was a joint project between NASA and the German Aerospace Center (DLR). It involved two satellites, GRACE-1 and GRACE-2, which tracked changes in Earth's gravity field to monitor variations in Earth's water resources, ice sheets, and climate.

Interesting fact: The GRACE mission discovered that Earth's ice sheets, such as those in Greenland and Antarctica, are losing mass at an accelerating rate, contributing to sea-level rise.

To find detailed information about the GRACE mission, you can visit the GRACE mission page on the NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) website.
